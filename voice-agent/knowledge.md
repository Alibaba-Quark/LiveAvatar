## Brand
- Lynk & Co is a Chinese-Swedish automotive brand, designed and engineered in
  Sweden, born from a Geely–Volvo collaboration and now part of the Geely /
  Zeekr group.
- Core idea: a premium, "always-on and connected" car — designed digital-first,
  with a large central touchscreen, cloud connectivity, an app-store-style
  ecosystem, and a modern, design-led identity.
- Powertrain focus in this region: plug-in hybrids (badged "EM-P") that pair a
  petrol engine with electric motors for long total range plus an electric-only
  daily range.

## Models (regional / Middle East lineup — confirm exact Jordan availability)

### Lynk & Co 900 (flagship — large luxury PHEV SUV)
- Three-row, **6-seat** full-size luxury SUV; the flagship of the range.
- Plug-in hybrid (EM-P). Headline versions produce up to ~**845 hp**.
- **~280 km electric-only range** (CLTC) from a ~52 kWh battery; **~1,443 km
  total combined range**.
- Notable: second-row seats with a 180° rotation feature; premium high-tech cabin.
- Positioned as a rival to large luxury SUVs.

### Lynk & Co 08 (mid-size PHEV SUV)
- Mid-size crossover SUV, plug-in hybrid (EM-P), available with AWD.
- Strong performance: top versions reach ~**612 hp**, 0–100 km/h in ~**4.6 s**.
- **~185 km electric-only range** (CLTC); up to ~**1,400 km** combined range.
- 1.5L turbo engine + electric motors; supports DC fast-charging.
- Roughly 4,820 mm long — a spacious family/premium SUV.

### Lynk & Co 06 (subcompact SUV)
- 5-seat subcompact crossover SUV; the most compact, accessible entry point.
- Petrol option: 1.5L turbo, ~**175 hp**, 7-speed dual-clutch.
- Plug-in hybrid (EM-P) option also available, ~**295 hp** combined.
- Around 4,340 mm long — easy, city-friendly footprint.

## Key selling points
- Connected, digital-first experience: large touchscreen, always-online cloud
  features, modern Scandinavian-influenced design.
- Plug-in hybrid efficiency: meaningful electric-only daily driving plus
  long-distance combined range, so no range anxiety.
- A premium feel and tech at a competitive position versus established luxury brands.
